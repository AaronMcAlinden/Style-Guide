Visual Marque

As part of our personal identity and personal brand development we were tasked with creating a visual marque to assist in the development of our visual identity. After an intensive period of sketch-noting & mind mapping session I came away with a few ideas for my brand. After a bit of a doodle session I began to develop this icon / illustration portrait of myself. I created this using the hexagonal shape from my monogram, as you can see a lot of the illustration has been made with different size hexagons. I also wanted to incorporate a reference to my love of illustration, thus the pen nib beard and the bezier curve handles and anchor point. I would like to try and animate this illustration at some stage, It could come in handy for use throughout my personal brand.

Monogram

I was tasked with creating a monogram to represent my own personal brand. At the beginning I drew a lot of inspiration from sources such as the Monogram Project on Instagram, as well as from the work of typographers and hand let- terers such as Jessica Hische and Marian Bantjes. I decided I wanted my monogram to have an illustrative feel. I began to look at the letters ‘A’ and ‘M’ as shapes, attempting to make them look like mountains or trees. Which eventually developed into my final design.

Wordmark

I began to look at colour combinations in unison with my monogram. I explored many colours, most of which I picked to reflect natural organic colours, some I just simply liked the look of. I finally arrived at a varia- tion of this colour palette. I really enjoyed the muted, organic, autum- nal, woodland feel these colours achieve. I spent a lot of time chopping and changing these colours. I finally decided upon this combination of colours as I wanted something a little more vibrant with more energy. I chose energetic colours to reflect my own personality and interests and to inspire creativity.

Body

I decided to use a simple slab serif typeface for my wordmark and throughout my brand material. I thought this style reflected myself and my brand well as slab serif typefaces have a certain rustic, warm, old fashioned feel to them. For the body of my text and for tertiary headings I decided to use a variation of a Futura typeface. Futura PT from Adobe typekit. I chose this particular font as it has a very clean yet modern look to it.
